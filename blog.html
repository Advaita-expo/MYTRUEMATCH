Compatibility

"We were matched by two families. We almost said yes - until the compatibility report."

Priya, 29, and Arjun, 32, were introduced through their respective families in Bangalore in early 2024. Both families were enthusiastic. On paper everything matched - community, profession, horoscope signs. But Priya's mother requested a full compatibility assessment before fixing a date.

The report revealed a fundamental difference in life-goal alignment: Arjun had ranked financial security as his primary value, while Priya had ranked emotional expression and creative freedom. Neither was wrong - but together, without awareness, this would have been a silent source of friction for years.

"The report didn't say don't marry him. It gave us a map. We had five real conversations in the next two weeks that we might never have had otherwise. Today we're married and those conversations are the reason we actually understand each other." - Priya, Bangalore

Verification

"His profile was polished. The gap was real."

Meena's father was retired from the IAS. He was careful with introductions. When a bio-data arrived from Dubai - software professional, MBA from a reputed college, clean family background - he still asked for verification. Not because he suspected anything. Because he had a principle: never assume.

The verification report revealed a 4-year employment gap between 2017 and 2021 that had been presented as continuous employment. When the family asked directly, the match acknowledged that he had spent two of those years managing a failed business venture he had felt embarrassed to mention.

Meena's family appreciated the honesty once it came. They proceeded, but with open eyes. "We didn't cancel the match. We had a real conversation. That would not have happened without the report." The wedding took place in June 2024.

"Not to catch him lying. To give him a chance to be honest."

- Meena's father, Chennai - June 2024

Horoscope

"Two broken engagements, one chart pattern."

Kavitha, 31, had called off an engagement in 2021. Rajiv, 34, had done the same in 2022. When their families were introduced in early 2024, both were cautious. A full Vedic chart reading was commissioned with N.R. Ranjith Kumar.

The reading identified a specific Navamsa configuration - Shukra in the 12th house for both - associated with delayed and tested unions, rather than failed ones. The astrologer explained that previous engagements likely broke because the timing was not aligned with this pattern, not because either person was the wrong match.

This reframing mattered enormously. Both had been carrying guilt. The chart gave them language for what had happened. They married in March 2025 and credit the reading as the single most meaningful moment in their journey together.

"He gave us a reason to stop apologising for our past and start building our future."

- Kavitha, Coimbatore - March 2025

Compatibility

"The report asked questions we were afraid to ask each other."

Ananya, 27, a clinical psychologist from Hyderabad, was introduced to Vikram, 30, an architect from Pune. They had met twice. Both families were keen to move forward quickly.

Ananya herself requested the compatibility assessment - unusual for the initiating side. The report identified a high alignment on emotional values and intellectual depth, but a divergence on extended family integration: Vikram expected his wife to live with his parents; Ananya had not considered this.

"I actually knew this was going to be the issue. But seeing it written in a neutral report made it easier to bring up. We negotiated a plan before we got married - not after." They wed in December 2024.

"It said out loud what we were both thinking quietly."

- Ananya & Vikram - December 2024

Verification

"NRI profile - no way to independently verify anything."

The Krishnamurthy family from Mysore received a proposal for their daughter from a Tamil family settled in Toronto. Education, career, values - all appeared excellent. But verifying anything about a person living abroad felt impossible for a family without contacts there.

MyTrueMatch's verification team confirmed the candidate's employment at a Toronto-based firm, his educational credentials from University of Waterloo, and conducted a civil records check across both Indian and Canadian databases. The report came back clean with verified documentation attached.

"We would have had to take everything on trust. Instead we could say yes from a place of knowledge, not hope." The engagement was fixed in August 2024.

"We said yes knowing, not hoping."

- Krishnamurthy family, Mysore - 2024

Compatibility

"We thought we were perfect. The report said 'mostly' - and that 'mostly' saved us."

Sunita, 26, and Dhruv, 28, both from Delhi, had been introduced through a common friend and had spoken regularly for two months before families were formally introduced. They felt aligned. The assessment was almost a formality.

The report rated their emotional and intellectual compatibility at 91% - but flagged a significant divergence in financial behaviour. Sunita was a natural saver; Dhruv had a history of impulse spending. Neither had noticed because they hadn't yet shared finances.

"We set up a joint account system before we married. It wasn't romantic but it was real. Two years later we have no money arguments - the only couples I know who can say that." Married November 2023.

"The 9% was the most important part of the whole report."

- Sunita & Dhruv - Delhi - November 2023
